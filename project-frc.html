FRC 2026 Season

This season's challenge centers on projectile-based scoring. Teams design and build robots capable of collecting and launching balls into designated goal zones.

2026 FRC Official Playing Field Layout

Game Overview

Competition Season: January – April 2026

Two alliances, each consisting of three robots, compete head-to-head. The objective is to collect game balls from the field and score them into goals. The alliance with the highest point total at the end of the match wins.

Game Balls

Foam balls are distributed across the field. Robots intake these balls and launch them toward scoring zones.

Scoring Zones

Each alliance has a high goal and low goal. The high goal offers greater point value but requires more precision.

Match Structure

Each match lasts 2 minutes and 30 seconds, divided into three distinct phases:

1. Autonomous Period

Duration: 15 seconds
Robots operate independently using pre-programmed routines. No driver input is permitted.

2. Teleoperated Period

Duration: 2 minutes
Drivers control robots to collect balls and score as efficiently as possible.

3. Endgame

Duration: Final 30 seconds
Robots climb onto elevated structures or park in designated zones for bonus points.

Scoring Breakdown

Goal Scoring
  • High Goal: 5 points per ball
  • Low Goal: 2 points per ball
  • Autonomous Bonus: Points are doubled during the autonomous period
Ball Management
  • Balls are available at loading stations and on the field
  • Robots may hold up to 5 balls simultaneously
  • Human players assist by feeding balls at designated stations
Endgame Scoring
  • Park: 3 points
  • Low Climb: 6 points
  • High Climb: 12 points
Ranking Points
  • Victory: 2 ranking points
  • Tie: 1 ranking point
  • Scoring Threshold: 30+ balls scored
  • Climb Bonus: All 3 alliance robots climb
